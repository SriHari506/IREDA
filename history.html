Our History

The Indian Renewable Energy Development Agency Limited (IREDA) is a Public Limited Government Company established as a Non-Banking Financial Institution in 1987. The objective of IREDA is to promote, develop, and extend financial assistance for renewable energy and energy efficiency/conservation projects.

1987: The Beginning

IREDA was established on 11th March 1987 as a Public Limited Government Company. Since its inception, IREDA has been committed to financing and promoting various renewable energy projects across India, focusing on sustainability and innovation.

1990s: Expansion and Growth

In the 1990s, IREDA expanded its portfolio to include a wider range of renewable energy projects such as wind energy, small hydro projects, and biomass power. The organization also started offering concessional financing to encourage the adoption of renewable energy technologies.

2000s: Diversification

With the turn of the millennium, IREDA diversified its activities further, financing energy efficiency and conservation projects alongside traditional renewable energy projects. The organization also played a significant role in implementing several national policies aimed at increasing the share of renewable energy in India's energy mix.

2010s: Recognition and Impact

In the 2010s, IREDA gained recognition for its impactful work and received several awards for its contributions to the renewable energy sector. The agency financed numerous large-scale projects, significantly contributing to India's renewable energy capacity.

Present: Leading the Way

Today, IREDA continues to lead the way in promoting and financing renewable energy projects in India. The organization is committed to supporting the country's goal of achieving 175 GW of renewable energy capacity by 2022 and aims to be a catalyst for India's transition to a sustainable and clean energy future.
